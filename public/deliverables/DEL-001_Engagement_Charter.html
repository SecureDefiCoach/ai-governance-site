1. Engagement Overview

Jones & Associates ("the Firm") has been engaged by Heritage Community Bank ("the Bank") to perform a Threat-Informed Cyber Risk Assessment. This engagement was initiated in response to three Matters Requiring Attention (MRAs) issued by the Federal Deposit Insurance Corporation ("FDIC") in a confidential supervisory communication dated March 28, 2026, following the Bank's information technology examination conducted January 12 – February 7, 2026.

The FDIC assigned a Composite IT Rating of 3 (Less Than Satisfactory) and cited the following MRAs:

MRAFinding
MRA 2026-IT-001Risk assessment methodology lacks threat intelligence integration — controls assessed against a static checklist without reference to adversary tactics, techniques, or procedures
MRA 2026-IT-002Board-level risk appetite for cybersecurity and emerging technology not formally articulated at the domain-specific granularity required to calibrate risk-taking decisions
MRA 2026-IT-003No independent cybersecurity assessment conducted within 36 months — most recent IT audit limited to financial statement general IT controls

The FDIC has directed the Board to demonstrate meaningful progress toward remediation within 90 days (risk assessment and appetite) and 120 days (independent assessment), with quarterly progress reporting thereafter.

The Board of Directors approved funding for a cybersecurity modernization initiative following the MRA letter, providing executive sponsorship for this engagement. The Firm has executed a Non-Disclosure Agreement for Confidential Supervisory Information (dated June 15, 2026) permitting access to the MRA letter for engagement scoping and execution.

3. Engagement Trigger

During the most recent regulatory examination, examiners issued the following MRA:

"The Bank's enterprise risk assessment relies on a checklist-based approach that does not adequately incorporate threat intelligence, map controls to specific threat scenarios relevant to the institution's risk profile, or align to a recognized threat framework. Management should enhance the risk assessment methodology to adopt a threat-informed approach consistent with the institution's size, complexity, and risk profile."

4. Scope

4.1 In Scope

  • Enterprise-wide threat-informed risk assessment covering all business lines, technology systems, and operational processes
  • MITRE ATT&CK technique profiling based on the Bank's threat landscape
  • CRI Profile v2.2 diagnostic statement evaluation across all applicable SCF control library domains
  • Control mapping and coverage analysis against selected threat techniques
  • Gap identification with risk-rated findings
  • Remediation roadmap with prioritized recommendations
  • Executive reporting and Board presentation

4.2 Out of Scope

  • Penetration testing or active vulnerability exploitation
  • Regulatory examination response drafting
  • Control implementation or remediation execution
  • Legal or compliance opinion
  • Business continuity plan testing or execution
  • Vendor contract negotiation or renegotiation

5. Methodology

The assessment follows a 15-stage engagement lifecycle aligned to the CRI Profile framework, MITRE ATT&CK, and NIST CSF 2.0:

  • 1Engagement PlanningDefine scope, timelines, stakeholders, communication cadence, and data handling requirements.
  • 2Business ContextUnderstand the Bank's business model, strategic priorities, regulatory environment, and risk appetite.
  • 3Threat ModelingIdentify threat actors, attack vectors, and scenarios relevant to the Bank's profile.
  • 4Threat IntelligenceGather current threat intelligence from public and industry sources (FS-ISAC, CISA, FBI IC3, FinCEN).
  • 5MITRE ATT&CK SelectionSelect applicable techniques based on the threat model and map to the Bank's technology environment.
  • 6CRI Profile MappingMap selected threat techniques to CRI Profile v2.2 diagnostic statements.
  • 7Evidence RequestIssue the information request list with evidence requirements and submission deadlines.
  • 8Evidence ValidationValidate quality, completeness, currency, and relevance of submitted evidence.
  • 9Coverage AnalysisAssess control coverage per CRI diagnostic statement. Score as Covered, Partial, or No Coverage.
  • 10Gap AnalysisIdentify gaps where controls do not adequately mitigate selected threat techniques.
  • 11Risk PrioritizationScore and prioritize findings based on likelihood, impact, and residual risk.
  • 12Threat Library AssemblyCompile the institution-specific Threat Library — the central deliverable of the engagement.
  • 13Executive ReportingProduce the engagement binder including executive summary, findings, and dashboards.
  • 14Remediation RoadmapDevelop a prioritized, phased remediation plan with estimated effort and quick wins.
  • 15Lessons LearnedDocument methodology improvements and recommendations for future assessment cycles.

10. Data Handling and Security

  • All Bank data stored in a dedicated AWS VPC
  • Access to data logged via AWS CloudTrail
  • No data leaves the VPC without explicit authorization
  • AI agent access is auditable — every query, read, and analysis action is logged
  • Evidence files stored in encrypted S3 buckets within the VPC
  • At engagement close: data destruction per NIST SP 800-88, CloudTrail deletion logs preserved, formal attestation issued
  • For production clients: access logs forwarded to the client in real-time

11. Assumptions

  1. The Bank will designate liaisons with authority to provide evidence and answer questions within agreed timelines
  2. Evidence will be provided in electronic format (PDF, Excel, Word, or screenshot) within 5 business days of request
  3. The Bank's existing risk assessment (checklist-based) will be provided as a baseline for comparison
  4. The Firm will have read-only access to relevant systems for evidence validation where applicable
  5. The assessment is advisory — the Firm does not implement controls or make binding risk acceptance decisions
  6. Findings are point-in-time based on evidence reviewed during the engagement period

12. Success Criteria

  1. A threat profile specific to the Bank's size, services, and geography has been developed and validated
  2. MITRE ATT&CK techniques relevant to the threat profile have been selected and mapped to CRI Profile diagnostic statements
  3. Coverage analysis has been completed across all applicable SCF control library domains with scoring at the response guidance level
  4. Gaps have been identified, risk-rated, and traced to specific MITRE mitigations
  5. A prioritized remediation roadmap has been delivered
  6. The Board has received an executive briefing on findings
  7. The engagement binder (17 deliverables) has been delivered
  8. All Bank data has been destroyed per the data handling protocol, with attestation provided
  9. The methodology is documented and repeatable for future assessment cycles
