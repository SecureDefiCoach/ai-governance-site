Dear Director,

Heritage Community Bank has engaged Jones & Associates to conduct an independent cyber risk assessment. A critical part of this work is understanding what the Board expects — not what management is currently doing, but what the Board believes the Bank should be doing.

This questionnaire asks for your perspective on how much cyber and technology risk the Bank should accept. There are no right or wrong answers. We are not testing your knowledge. We are asking for your judgment as a fiduciary.

Why Your Individual Response Matters

Regulators (OCC, FDIC) expect each board member to form an independent view of the institution's risk appetite. When we collect responses separately, we can identify where the Board agrees, where members see things differently, and where the Board's expectations may differ from management's current practices.

Those differences are among the most valuable findings in a risk assessment. They help the Bank focus resources where they matter most.

What Happens With Your Answers

1. Each board member completes this questionnaire independently — your responses are not shared with other members or with management.

2. Jones & Associates compiles all responses into a single Board Risk Appetite Summary showing consensus areas and areas of divergence. No individual names are attached.

3. That summary shapes our assessment priorities:

Areas where the Board wants low risk but controls are weak → receive the most scrutiny

Areas where the Board accepts higher risk → evaluated for appropriate documentation and regulatory defensibility

Areas of Board disagreement → flagged for discussion at a future Board session

4. The final assessment report will show how the Bank's actual cyber posture compares to the Board's stated appetite — giving you a clear picture of where the Bank stands relative to where you want it to be.

Example: If the Board selects "low appetite" for customer data exposure, but evidence shows weak data classification or encryption gaps, that mismatch becomes a priority finding in our assessment. Your answers set the standard we measure against.

Core vs. Optional Questions

Questions are divided into two tiers:

CORE  30 questions — These directly shape assessment priorities and risk scoring. Please answer all core questions.

OPTIONAL  37 questions — These provide additional depth. Answer as many as you wish.

Note: For optional questions, the directors who do respond will carry greater weight in shaping that area of the assessment. If a topic matters to you, answering the optional questions ensures your perspective is represented.

Time commitment: Core questions take approximately 15 minutes. The full questionnaire takes 25–30 minutes. You do not need to complete it in one sitting — you may save and return.

Important: Responses to this questionnaire are not risk acceptance decisions. They express the Board's appetite and expectations. Formal risk acceptance requires separate Board action through established governance processes. Management validation is required before responses are used to support assessment findings.
