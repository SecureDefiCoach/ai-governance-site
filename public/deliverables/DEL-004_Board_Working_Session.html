Decision 2 of 6

AI Governance Position

Question: Should AI tools be allowed to process confidential Bank or customer data?

No — under no circumstances (3)
Yes — Board controls (1)
Undecided (1)
Why this matters: Your vendors are already embedding AI into products the Bank uses. A blanket prohibition may not be enforceable. A controlled adoption framework provides governance without driving AI underground.

Proposed Resolution — Board Vote Required

Option A — Prohibit
No AI processes Bank data. Audit vendor contracts for existing AI. Accept that some vendor AI features may need to be disabled.
Option B (Recommended)
Prohibit today, but direct management to develop an AI Governance Framework for Board approval within 90 days. Framework gates all future AI decisions.
Option C — Controlled
Allow with Board-approved controls. Each AI use case requires a risk assessment, vendor review, and Board vote before deployment.
Decision 3 of 6

Risk Acceptance Authority

Question: Who should have authority to accept cyber or AI risk on behalf of the Bank?

Tiered: CISO → Risk Cmt → Board (3)
All via Risk Cmt (1)
Management only (1)
Why this matters: Examiners will ask: "Who approved this risk exception?" Without defined authority levels, the Bank cannot demonstrate functioning risk governance.

Proposed Resolution — Board Vote Required

Option A (Recommended)
Tiered authority mirroring lending limits:
Routine (≤$50K impact): CISO/CIO
Material ($50K-$250K): Risk Committee
Strategic (>$250K or policy): Full Board
Option B
All risk acceptance through Risk Committee. Ensures oversight but may create bottlenecks on routine operational exceptions.

10:25 AM — Board Motions

Proposed Board Resolutions

Based on today's discussion, Jones & Associates recommends the Board adopt the following resolutions. Each requires a formal vote and will be documented in the Board minutes.

1
RESOLVED — The Board adopts a near-zero tolerance for cybersecurity underinvestment and approves funding the CISO's FY2027 cybersecurity budget at or above peer benchmarks. (per option selected on Decision 1)
2
RESOLVED — The Board directs management to develop an AI Governance Framework within 90 days for Board approval. Until that framework is approved, no AI tools shall process confidential Bank or customer data. (per option selected on Decision 2)
3
RESOLVED — The Board adopts a tiered risk acceptance authority structure. CISO/CIO for routine exceptions (≤$50K), Risk Committee for material exceptions ($50K-$250K), Full Board for strategic risk decisions (>$250K or policy). (per option selected on Decision 3)
4
RESOLVED — The Board sets $750,000 as the materiality threshold for a single cyber event. Any event triggering regulatory notification is automatically material. This threshold shall be documented in the Risk Appetite Statement. (Decision 4)
5
RESOLVED — The Board directs the Compensation Committee to include cybersecurity competency in the Board skills matrix and to identify candidates with technology or cybersecurity background for future appointment. Annual Board cyber literacy training begins in Q4 2026. (Decision 5)
6
RESOLVED — The Board directs management to evaluate and strengthen the three lines of defense for cyber risk, including compliance reporting independence, internal audit IT scope, and first-line risk ownership. Report back within 60 days. (Decision 6)
