1. Purpose

This document defines the boundaries, constraints, and assessment parameters for the Threat-Informed Cyber Risk Assessment of Heritage Community Bank. It operationalizes the scope described in the Engagement Letter (HCB-EL-002) into specific assessment targets, exclusions, and success measures.

1.1 Regulatory Basis

This assessment directly responds to three FDIC Matters Requiring Attention issued March 28, 2026. The scope is designed so that each MRA is addressed by specific deliverables:

MRAFindingDeliverables
MRA 2026-IT-001Risk assessment lacks threat intelligence integrationDEL-008 — 013 (Assessment)
MRA 2026-IT-002Board risk appetite not formally articulatedDEL-004, 005 (Governance)
MRA 2026-IT-003No independent cybersecurity assessment (36 months)DEL-006 — 019 (Full engagement)

The FDIC has directed the Bank to demonstrate meaningful progress within 90 days (risk assessment methodology + Board risk appetite) and 120 days (independent assessment completion), with quarterly progress reporting thereafter. Engagement timelines are calibrated to these regulatory deadlines.

3. Exclusions

  1. Penetration testing or active vulnerability exploitation
  2. Regulatory examination response drafting
  3. Control implementation or remediation execution
  4. Legal or compliance opinion
  5. Business continuity plan testing or execution
  6. Vendor contract negotiation or renegotiation
  7. Trust services (outsourced, examined separately)
  8. Phone/VoIP systems
  9. Physical security / CCTV systems
  10. Personal devices (BYOD) — unless used for privileged access

5.2 Attack Surface

  • Internet-facing services (online/mobile banking, email, VPN)
  • Third-party connections (core processor, MSP, managed firewall)
  • End-user endpoints (phishing, malware, credential theft)
  • Privileged access paths (admin accounts, service accounts)
  • Physical access to data center and branch infrastructure
  • Remote worker access (VPN + MFA)
  • Wire/ACH transfer systems (FedLine Advantage)

5.3 MITRE ATT&CK Technique Selection Criteria

  1. Relevance to the identified threat actors
  2. Applicability to the Bank's technology environment
  3. Historical use against financial sector targets (per FS-ISAC, CISA, FBI IC3 advisories)
  4. Coverage of the full attack lifecycle (Initial Access through Impact)

Estimated technique count: 40-60 techniques and sub-techniques

6. Assessment Approach

6.1 Coverage Scoring

CoveredControl exists, is documented, and evidence demonstrates effective operation
PartialControl exists but has gaps in design, implementation, or evidence
No CoverageNo control exists, or no evidence provided to demonstrate the control

Coverage percentages will be reported in 25% bands (0% / 25% / 50% / 75% / 100%).

6.2 Gap Traceability

Every identified gap must trace to:

  1. The specific CRI diagnostic statement that is not met
  2. The MITRE ATT&CK technique(s) the control should mitigate
  3. The specific MITRE mitigation the gap fails to address
  4. The evidence (or absence of evidence) supporting the finding

Gaps based on best-practice opinion without mitigation traceability will not be included in findings.

6.3 Risk Scoring

RatingScore Range
Critical20-25

Likelihood (1-5) based on threat intelligence. Impact (1-5) based on CIA impact to operations, customers, and regulatory standing. Risk Score = Likelihood x Impact.

7.2 Evidence Quality Standards

  • Current (within 12 months unless otherwise justified)
  • Complete (covers the full scope of the control)
  • Authentic (from the Bank's systems, not recreated)
  • Relevant (directly supports the diagnostic statement)

Stale, incomplete, or contradictory evidence will be flagged for follow-up before conclusions are drawn.

8. Constraints and Assumptions

Constraints

  1. Assessment is evidence-based — no system access beyond read-only where granted
  2. Findings are point-in-time as of the assessment period
  3. The Firm does not make risk acceptance decisions
  4. All Bank data destroyed at engagement close per NIST 800-88

Assumptions

  1. Bank liaisons respond to evidence requests within 5 business days
  2. The Bank's existing checklist-based risk assessment will be provided as baseline
  3. Stakeholders are available for interviews as scheduled
  4. The Bank's technology environment does not change materially during the assessment period
